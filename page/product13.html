Product description

Enjoy a fast and fun floor cleaning experience with our #1 Spin Mop*, which features hands-free wringing thanks to our foot pedal spin mop design. The foot pedal on the bucket allows for hands-free wringing, so you have less mess when mopping. Control the level of moisture of your mop with the built-in high-quality foot pedal designed to activate spin wringing. The EasyWring Spin Bucket & Mop gives you the ability to control the amount of water and moisture applied to floors making for easier wringing and quicker-drying floors. The deep-cleaning microfiber removes and absorbs tough dirt and grime leaving floors clean and dust free.

*The EasyWring #1 Spin Mop claim is based on IRI, Total US MULO, L52wks 9/9/18, Consumer Focused Durable Mops dollar sales.

1.0 out of 5 starsTLDR: Product is tiny and cheap. Suspect fake reviews.
September 11, 2018
Verified Purchase
4.5 stars. Nearly five thousand reviews. 30 bucks. Might as well give it a shot right? Yeah, this thing reminds me of one those toy janitor sets. It's just so small and cheap. Judging by the pictures, it looks like the mop handle is supposed to have 3 segments to extend. I was only able to extend 2. I thought about taking channel locks to the thing in order get that supposed third segment unstuck, but I figured I was going to return any way; thus, I didn't want to damage it.
Regardless, the mop head is laughably small. It has about a third of the material that you'd find on the typical household mop. Once again, I was planning on returning it, so I did not test how well the head actually cleaned; however, I remain rather skeptical.
Oh, and I'm not an expert on such things, but I suspect that the picture of the lady using the product either featured a really tiny girl, or a bit of photoshop, in order to make the product look bigger. The size difference may not be as egregious, but it reminds me of those pictures of kiddy pools you sometimes see floating around social media. You know, the one's where the box art makes the product look huge, but it's actually really tiny.
Taking a closer look at the reviews, other people seem to be complaining about the mop wringer breaking as well. All the gears for that look solid enough; so I don't know if the plastic is rather brittle, or if they thickened it up with a later revision. Regardless, plastic mechanical parts typically aren't known for having long mean times between failures, and people are reporting that it is a point of failure. So, consider it another reason to shy away from the product -especially since I could find no information about a warranty on Amazon nor O-Cedar's website.
And finally, after being surprised at how dinky the thing was, I decided to take a look at what people had actually written, and saw a review with someone mentioning "Fakespot", which is a site meant to identify fake reviews. Well hell, I've got to check this out. Looks, like old Fakespot says that the aggregate reviews have an "A" grade, but it also indicates that Amazon removed about 2,000 reviews on two separate occasions. Now, I don't know why Amazon decided to purge nearly 4k reviews, but all of this stuff makes me strongly suspect a fair bit of review astroturfing.
Needless to say, I will be returning this dinky piece of garbage. Moreover, the thing makes me think that the O-Cedar business model is all about selling garbage to consumers, and hoping that a minimal amount will be bothered to return it. Stay away from the mop. Stay away from the O-Cedar brand.

5.0 out of 5 starsMuch Better Than My Old "Mop"
June 11, 2017
Verified Purchase
My Amazon review for my new O-Cedar Easy-Wring Microfiber mop:

I need to qualify my review to disclose that I am blessed to be in a position to pay someone else to mop my floors. She couldn't make it over a few weeks ago (I think she's in her 70s so quite frankly I'm surprised she still comes at all) so I attempted to mop the floor myself. In my (i.e., her) utility closet I found a mop pole with a sponge tied on to it with a couple of rolled up plastic bags. Literally. I can't tell if this is what she has been using this whole time but considering that I don't remember EVER buying a mop, much less a replacement head for it, there is no other less mortifying reality. I used it, had to repeatedly adjust the sponge and plastic bag rope, did a horrible job, dropped it on the floor and jumped online to order this mop ASAP. So my disclosure is that I'd be giving five stars to literally anything better than a sponge on a stick.

I just used this O-Cedar for the first time. Loved it. Assembled quickly and easily. Uses salad-spinner technology with a foot pedal. Genius! The dampness is customizable (step on the gas for dryer sponge.) Again, I'm hardly a mop expert but it appears to me that keeping the mop head (if that's what you even call it) only lightly damp allows you to exert more pressure to pick up more stubborn grime without creating puddles. Plus the floor dries faster. Then to take off mop head, simply stand on some strands and lift handle up and the head pops right off. Machine washable but lay out to dry (although I haven't done that yet so can't comment on how clean it gets.) And then there's the bonus feature of doubling as a foot-operated salad spinner.

Careful on wood floors not to go to fast or else strands bunch up into a "tail" causing you to scrape your floor when pushing forward.

My toddler daughter and grandson (don't ask) were so bewildered at Mom/Grandma doing so much manual labor that they were genuinely curious. They literally fought over the mop and took turns mopping. This, needless to say, is a testament to how easy the mop is to use (and how bad of a mother/grandmother I am.)

My cleaning lady came back last week and I hugged her and gave her a raise. Although now having tried the O-Cedar Easy-Wring Microfiber Mop I am considering letting my cleaning lady finally retire so I may capitalize on this free child labor. I will definitely be looking into other O-Cedar products to get for their respective birthdays.
